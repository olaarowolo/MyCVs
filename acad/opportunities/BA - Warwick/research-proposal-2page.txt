Media, Poverty, and Democracy: A Comparative Analysis of Social Investment Discourse in the UK and Nigeria

Research Overview
This comparative study examines how media narratives around poverty and social investment shape democratic accountability and public engagement in the UK and Nigeria. Building on my PhD research on Nigeria's National Social Investment Programmes (NSIP), this project offers a unique North-South perspective on media's role in democratic processes.

Research Questions
1. How do mainstream and digital media in the UK and Nigeria frame poverty and social investment programmes?
2. What ideological and discursive strategies are used in media portrayals of welfare?
3. How do these narratives influence public trust, policy reception, and civic participation?
4. How can we develop a cross-cultural framework for understanding media's role in democratic accountability?

Methodology
Comparative qualitative design integrating:
- Content analysis of major newspapers, TV transcripts, and digital platforms (2015-2025)
- Critical discourse analysis of welfare reporting focusing on metaphors and beneficiary representation
- Elite interviews with 15-20 journalists, policy experts, and NGO advocates from both countries

Expected Outcomes
- Typology of media frames around social investment programmes
- Cross-cultural insights into poverty discourse politicization
- Digital dashboard tracking cross-country media discourse trends
- Policy briefs for media professionals and social policy actors

Timeline (24 months)
Months 1-3: Literature review and framework development
Months 4-7: Media sampling and data collection (UK & Nigeria)
Months 8-12: Coding and preliminary analysis
Months 13-15: Conduct interviews
Months 16-18: Discourse analysis and interpretation
Months 19-21: Dashboard and policy brief development
Months 22-24: Writing, dissemination, and final reporting

Alignment with British Academy Priorities
This research directly supports themes of "Democracy, Conflict and Governance" and "Justice, Rights and Equality" while strengthening UK-Global South research partnerships through interdisciplinary, policy-relevant scholarship.
