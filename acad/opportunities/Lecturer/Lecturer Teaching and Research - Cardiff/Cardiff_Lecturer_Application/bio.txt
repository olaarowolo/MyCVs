**Bio: Olasunkanmi Arowolo**
Dr. Olasunkanmi Sunday Arowolo is a recent PhD graduate from the University of Kent's Centre for Journalism, where he successfully defended his thesis with no corrections. He specializes in media sociology and explores research in digital journalism, citizen journalism, and environmental communication, with particular expertise in poverty discourse and social investment programs in Nigeria. He has over eight years of experience as a media and communication scholar and journalist, including roles as Assistant Lecturer at Lagos State University and currently as Lecturer at the University of Kent. Dr. Arowolo has published extensively in leading journals including Global Knowledge, Memory and Communication (Emerald Publishing), Cogent Social Sciences (Taylor & Francis), and East African Journal of Interdisciplinary Studies. He is a member of the Public Communication of Science and Technology (PCST), International Communication Association (ICA) and the Association of Communication Scholars and Professionals of Nigeria (ACSPN), and Association of Media and Communication Researchers of Nigeria(AMCRON). His professional experience as a journalist and academic informs his research on media representation of social issues, and both directly influence his teaching in journalism and communication studies. He has presented research at various national and international conferences.

Dr. Arowolo's research impact is reflected in over 260 citations, with an h-index of 5 and i10-index of 2, demonstrating his growing influence in the fields of Journalism, Media Sociology, Media and Democracy, News Framing, and Development Communication. His key publications include foundational works on framing theory, media audience analysis, and journalism ethics, with recent contributions addressing the dilemmas and social constraints in journalism ethics. His scholarly work continues to gain recognition, particularly since 2020, highlighting his active engagement with contemporary media and communication challenges.
