## 1. Project Title  
**"Sustainable Journalism Ecosystems in Nigeria: Funding Models, Innovation and Policy for Media Resilience"**

---

## 2. Executive Summary  
This project investigates the funding models, sustainability mechanisms and policy frameworks that underpin independent journalism in Nigeria. Using a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative content analysis, qualitative interviews and participatory action research, the study will map the financial ecosystems of Nigerian newsrooms, assess the impact of digital disruption on journalistic independence and propose evidence-based policy recommendations to strengthen media resilience. The project directly addresses TETFund's priorities for media development, entrepreneurship and national innovation systems.

---

## 3. Background and Rationale  
### 3.1 Context  
Nigeria's media landscape has undergone rapid transformation due to digital disruption, economic pressures and evolving audience behaviors. Independent journalism faces challenges including:
- **Funding instability** from declining advertising revenues
- **Digital disruption** affecting traditional revenue models
- **Policy gaps** in media sustainability frameworks
- **Innovation needs** for sustainable business models

### 3.2 Problem Statement  
Despite Nigeria's vibrant media sector, independent journalism faces existential threats from:
- **Revenue decline** of 40-60% in traditional media over the past decade
- **Limited access** to sustainable funding mechanisms
- **Policy gaps** in supporting media innovation
- **Innovation deficits** in digital business models

### 3.3 Significance  
This study will:
- **Strengthen media sustainability** through evidence-based policy recommendations
- **Support entrepreneurship** by identifying viable business models
- **Enhance innovation systems** through digital transformation strategies
- **Contribute to policy development** for media sector resilience

---

## 4. Objectives  

### 4.1 Primary Objective  
To investigate funding models, sustainability mechanisms and policy frameworks for independent journalism in Nigeria, with a focus on digital transformation and entrepreneurship.

### 4.2 Specific Objectives  
1. **Map** the financial ecosystems of Nigerian newsrooms across print, broadcast and digital platforms
2. **Assess** the impact of digital disruption on journalistic independence and sustainability
3. **Identify** innovative business models and funding mechanisms for media sustainability
4. **Develop** evidence-based policy recommendations for media sector resilience
5. **Create** a digital dashboard for tracking media sustainability indicators

---

## 5. Methodology  

### 5.1 Research Design  
**Mixed-methods approach** combining:
- **Quantitative:** Content analysis of 500+ news outlets (2020-2025)
- **Qualitative:** 50 in-depth interviews with editors, publishers, funders
- **Participatory:** 5 focus groups with journalists and entrepreneurs
- **Digital:** Computational analysis of 10,000+ social media posts

### 5.2 Study Population  
- **Primary:** 100 independent newsrooms across Nigeria (print, broadcast, digital)
- **Secondary:** 200 journalists, 50 funders, 25 policy makers
- **Tertiary:** 1,000 audience members via surveys

### 5.3 Data Collection Methods  
- **Surveys:** Online questionnaires for newsrooms and journalists
- **Interviews:** Semi-structured interviews with key stakeholders
- **Focus Groups:** Participatory sessions with media entrepreneurs
- **Document Analysis:** Financial reports, policy documents, media content
- **Digital Analytics:** Social media and website analytics

### 5.4 Data Analysis  
- **Quantitative:** Statistical analysis using SPSS/R for survey data
- **Qualitative:** Thematic analysis using NVivo for interview data
- **Digital:** Computational text analysis using Python/R
- **Policy:** Policy analysis framework for recommendations
