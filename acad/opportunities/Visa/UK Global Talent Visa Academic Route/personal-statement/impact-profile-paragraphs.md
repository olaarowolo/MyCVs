# Impact Profile: Dr. Sunday Arowolo

Dr. Olasunkanmi Arowolo stands as a pioneering journalism scholar-practitioner who achieved the rare distinction of completing his doctoral thesis with zero amendments zero amendments required—placing him among the elite 5% of British doctoral candidates. This exceptional accomplishment crystallizes his position at the vanguard of contemporary media scholarship.

His research fundamentally transforms understanding of media's role in African democracy and poverty governance. Through pioneering mixed-methods studies on Nigeria's National Social Investment Programmes, he has established new theoretical frameworks that bridge traditional journalism and digital activism in emerging democracies. His work has garnered 262 Google Scholar citations across three continents, validating his global academic influence.

As a British Academy-GCRF Research Assistant on the Pneuma-City project between 2022 to 2023, he contributed to the £1.2 million research programme on urban infrastructures of well-being. His international teaching excellence spans the University of Kent and Lagos State University, where he has demonstrated sustained commitment to advancing media education. He conducted the first comprehensive study interviewing Nigerian journalists on social welfare coverage and developed policy recommendations that is well-positioned to be adopted by Nigerian government agencies.

Currently, he leads two national studies examining Nigerian journalism and political media coverage. He founded the Research Africa platform, which democratises access to Global South scholarship and has garnered national media recognition from Nigeria's leading outlets.

His work positions African media scholarship at the forefront of global discourse while maintaining practical relevance for policy and democratic development. He advances evidence-based approaches to media governance, ensuring African contexts inform international theoretical paradigms. His future vision encompasses establishing the first African Media Policy Research Centre to strengthen UK-African scholarly collaboration and advance inclusive digital humanities research globally.
