Ongoing Research

Bridging the Gap Between Scientific Research and Public Engagement in Nigeria: Rethinking Media Practices in the Digital Age

Abstract

Science, technology, and innovation (STI) communications play a significant socio-economic role in the transformative process of every modern society. Although there have been several research outputs (by individuals, groups, or institutes) across health, agriculture, technology, and social sciences, a significant gap persists between research production and public engagement. This study inquires into the role of media in mediating public access to research outputs with a focus on the challenges posed by limited editorial capacity, politicised media ownership, and the digital misinformation ecosystem.

This study uses the mixed methods approach (content analysis and in-depth interviews with journalists, researchers, and science communicators). It explores current practices and identifies the gaps in the mass media coverage of STI-related content.

The results of this study will be presented at the first International Conference on Science Communication at the African University of Science and Technology, FCT Abuja, Nigeria, October 2025.
